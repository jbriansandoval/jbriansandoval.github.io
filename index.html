About Me

My name is Jose Brian Sandoval; however, I prefer Brian, my middle name. When given the opportunity, I tend to exceed expectations and complete every task to the best of my ability. I find patterns and problems; then deliver a reliable solution.

  • Growing up...

    1999-2001

    When I was 3 my family started a small business. They had a small shop that opened only on the weekends in Fletcher, North Carolina.

    2 years later they decided to open a second business in Waynesville, NC. This Store would focus on providing cultural foods to our Hispanic community.

  • 2001-2004

    La Mexicanita

    Our business became our life and as I grew older, our business grew. We expanded twice, and soon as I learned to count change, I begged to learn the cash register.

  • 2004-2009

    Expansion

    At 12 years old, I started counting the register. For each service we had, we adopted a separate system for doing it. By this point I started signing up for computer classes at school and learning Microsoft Word and Excel.

    The older I got, the more I was eager to learn. By 14 started counting deposits and learning the end of the day reports. I began to make small changes where I thought we could improve. I also began learning the new systems first and then train the rest of the team.

  • 2009-2013

    High School

    By this point, our business had become my life. I came straight from school and helped my parents every day. At 16 years old, I was running the business and closing the store. My parents could now leave and have dinner alone or take a night off.

    I became the person to implement changes across the business; whether a new system or services, I was the one to make it happen. This passion has drove me to pursue a degree in Business Administration.

  • 2013-2017

    Johnson & Wales University

    As a Business major, gained the ability to pursue many different opportunities. Working at Marriott has provided me just that, an opportunity.

    My education has provided me with a foundation of fundamental business principles, that I have seen first-hand through my work experience. Because of my drive and experience, my roles in the work place have always evolved and become more operations driven.

  • 2016-Today

    Marriott International

    Working here at the Charlotte Marriott City Center, as a Room Controller, I have learned so much in a very short period. I started in July of 2016, during the final stages of a two-year renovation project.

    Our “Beta” hotel as we call it, has provided me with a tremendous amount of experience. Every day is different, and I am often challenged in a new way. New systems, new projects, new groups, I am always excited for what is next. I now possess a much better understanding of how much goes into running a hotel, especially the hotel of the future.

  • My Next Step:
    Learning Collaborator

"I ASPIRE TO BE ON THE TEAM THAT TAKES THE BRAND INTO THE FUTURE."

Throughout my experiences, many of my roles have been related to customer service. Working both face to face and over the phone, I've learned to think as I go. Most people freeze in challenging situations, but I can remain calm and use caution when making a decision.

Growing up, I was never taught to just do a job. I was groomed, by two of the smartest entrepreneurs I know, to take over the business. I quickly learned that no one is going to do your work for you.

If you forget to do something, it's just going to be there until you do it. It's easier if you just do it, and do it right, the first time. The most reliable people are the ones who take the initiative.

In today's world, most people wait to be rewarded before they do even think about doing. I've realized that the harder you work the bigger the reward. It may not be soon, or even in the current situation, but If you exceed your goals you will be rewarded.

I am motivated by knowing I did the best I could, and I made a difference. Whether I know someone noticed, or not, I know I left it better for the person after me.

Working in such a unique role, I never stop learning. I've realized that asking why, is the only way to understand something. If you don't ask why, you won't know how it happened or how to prevent it from happening again.

Nothing can really scare you when you know how it works.

I find joy in developing a new way to do something. If I think something will work better a different way, I try it. If it doesn't work, I try something else. A good strategy is always changing with every new situation that arises.

If you don't better your way of doing, someone else will do it better than you.

Naturally I am interested in how something works. Because of this curiosity, I enjoy learning new concepts and ideas.

This has been essential when working at a property so unique as the Charlotte Marriott City Center. Our "Beta" Hotel is one of the first to try new concepts being developed throughout the brand.
